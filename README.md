#AugMentor 2.0 – AI-Assisted Craft Skill Analysis

AugMentor 2.0 is an AI-powered system designed to help learners improve manual craft skills by analyzing and comparing their movements with those of an expert.

The system processes egocentric craft videos and uses computer vision and AI models to extract motion information, analyze gesture quality, and generate meaningful feedback for the learner.

This project is part of a Final Year Project developed in collaboration with researchers from Mines Paris – PSL University.

Project Idea

Learning complex manual skills such as carving, sculpting, or crafting often relies on observing experts and practicing repeatedly. However, many critical aspects of these movements are subtle and difficult to perceive in standard videos.

AugMentor 2.0 aims to transform instructional videos into interactive learning tools by automatically extracting motion information and highlighting the key elements of expert performance.

By comparing a learner’s performance with an expert’s demonstration, the system can identify differences in motion and provide explanations that help improve technique.

The goal is to make tacit motor knowledge visible and understandable through AI-driven analysis and visualization.

Core Concept

The system analyzes two videos:

Expert video – demonstration of the correct technique

Learner video – attempt performed by the student

Using computer vision and AI models, the system:

Detects hand and object movements in the video

Extracts motion information such as trajectories and gesture patterns

Compares the learner’s motion with the expert’s motion

Generates explanations and visual feedback to guide improvement

This approach turns simple demonstration videos into augmented learning material.

Technologies

The project explores the combination of several AI technologies:

Computer Vision for hand and object detection

Motion analysis for gesture quality metrics

Vision-Language Models for generating explanations

Video augmentation techniques for pedagogical visualization

These components work together to convert raw videos into structured motion data and teaching feedback.

Research Context

AugMentor 2.0 extends an earlier prototype by introducing new AI models and analytics capable of extracting motion indicators from craft videos and producing annotated learning material.

The project explores how machine learning and visualization can improve the teaching and understanding of dexterous manual skills.
